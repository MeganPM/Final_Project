Solution to our teachers problem. It’ll blow your mind.


The pay of a teacher in Mexico varies but is one of the worst (from $6,679 to $8,155 per month). This is the origin of our first problem: the lack of interest in teaching. The solution is to increase their pay so their quality of life will be better and the interest will increase too.
The other problem is that some teachers are not qualified to teach their subjects, that could be solved by giving summer courses.

Lazy Students? See the solution for yourself.

Students see school as a boring thing their parents force them. They see it as a hell where the only thing you do is read books and take notes, and with this they had lost interest in school. In the other hand, their parents force them to work; so by this way, the students are loosing a path to knowledge, not the best but it is better than nothing. In other cases, there can be students who really are bad at school. Our solution is that schools will provide extra-curricular activities to make school fun.

And lastly, schools. Checkmate.

The deterioration and poor conditions in the physical infrastructure of the educational centers continues to be one of the unresolved problems in the agenda of the Secretary of Education
• The lack of columns and the collapse of the floors of the schools endanger more than a thousand students who come every day to receive their classes.
• 30% of the schools are difficult to access because some of them are in hills or mountains, or because to reach it the students have to cross rivers.
• 10% of students attend some of the 15,600 rural schools in the country. These represent 38% of the total establishments. Only 28% of these are at the secondary level.
• The development of physical education infrastructure has been deficient, insufficient and inadequate, a situation that violates the right of girls, boys and adolescents to quality education. .
